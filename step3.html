Many people consider the filiming the easiest part of the entire process. They would be wrong. Filming is actually one of the hardest parts! If someone screws up one thing, then the whole movie could spiral out of control. Luckily, there are simple rules you can follow to keep yourself in good shape before and during filming.

Rule #1: Check your budget and pick your locations. If you plan to use no budget, look for places that will allow you to film for free like a school, a community school, hospital, or your home. Also, recruit friends to be actors (they may be not a-list actors but they are cheap!) and find everyday items for props. If you have a budget, look for locations that are afforadable that will not take up too much of your budget, which will be neede for props, costumes, and actors.
Rule #2: Focus on one spot at a time. While it could be filmed chronogically, I would recommend doing all the filming for one location. Do it as quickly possible because the weather and time of day changes faster than most people think and can make your movie scenes appear different due to the lighting. Take multiple days if you have to but don't take too long (especially if you have a deadline). Start with the easiest accessible location possible. Save your hardest location last so you can focus on all your attention on that.
Rule #3: If you need to redo any scenes, do it quickly but carefully (rushing on your work is a terrible idea). You still need to leave time for post-production.
Rule #4: Don't panic. If anything bad happens during filming, stay calm and handle it with a positive attitude. Also, try to have fun while filming. Otherwise, what is the point?
